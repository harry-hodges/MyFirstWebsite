Accessibility Solutions On My Website

Colour Blindness

Research shows that 99% of colour blind people have red-green colour blindness. This means they have difficulty distinguishing between these two colours. To accommodate for this, on my quiz page, I opted for using blue to indicate an incorrect answer and green for a correct answer. For more information on colourblindness, follow this link: Colourblind Guide.

Use of Smaller Screens

The website is compatable at a minimum width of 320px, meaning that it will run on the users smartphones.

Physical Disabilities

There is no time limit on the quiz, or any other page on the website. The page can also be fully navigated using the keyboard.

Speech Disabilities

The user has a choice of how they contact me. They can copy my email and send me an email in a format they are comfortable with, or they can fill out the form and press submit.

Blindness

The photos in the quiz have been explained in the alt section of the img tag. This along with a speech reader would allow a blind person to take the quiz, and understand other photos used throughout the website.

Other Accessibility Issues

Blindness

A very popular and reliable speech reader is JAWS. It is the world's most popular windows screen reader, and is used and recommended by one of the most famous blind influencers called Toby (@blindtobes on tiktok). More infomation about JAWS can be found at this link: JAWS Website.
